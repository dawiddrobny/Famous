Adam Małysz

Adam Małysz is a legendary Polish ski jumper and one of the most successful athletes in the history of the sport. Born on December 3, 1977, in Wisła, he became a national hero in Poland, sparking a phenomenon known as "Małyszomania" during the early 2000s. Throughout his illustrious career, he achieved numerous milestones, including four individual World Championship titles—an all-time record—and four overall World Cup titles.

Małysz's success was defined by his incredible consistency and technique on both normal and large hills. He won a total of 39 individual World Cup competitions and stood on the podium 92 times in individual events. Over four Winter Olympics, he earned four individual medals, including three silver and one bronze, solidifying his status as an elite competitor on the global stage.

After retiring from ski jumping in 2011, Małysz pursued a second career as a rally driver, successfully completing the Dakar Rally multiple times. Today, he remains a prominent figure in the sports world, serving as the President of the Polish Ski Federation. His legacy continues to inspire generations of young athletes in Poland and across the world of winter sports.

Timeline of Adam Małysz's Life

  • 1977: Born on December 3 in Wisła, Poland.
  • 1983: Made his first ski jump at the age of six.
  • 1994: Joined the Polish national ski jumping team.
  • 1996: Earned his first World Cup victory in Oslo.
  • 2000-2001: Won the Four Hills Tournament and his first overall World Cup title.
  • 2002: Won two Olympic medals (silver and bronze) in Salt Lake City.
  • 2003: Became the first male ski jumper to win three consecutive World Cup titles.
  • 2007: Claimed his fourth overall World Cup title.
  • 2010: Won two silver medals at the Vancouver Winter Olympics.
  • 2011: Officially retired from professional ski jumping.
  • 2012-2014: Competed in the Dakar Rally, finishing as high as 13th overall.
  • 2022: Elected as the President of the Polish Ski Federation.
